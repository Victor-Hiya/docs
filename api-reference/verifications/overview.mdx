Verifications are performed on [audios][2] to analyze and
measure certain characteristics, which vary depending on the
type of verification.

The generic verifications endpoint provides a unified view
of all the different types, displaying only their common fields.

<Note>
  **Access:** To manage a verification,
  you must have access to its [space][1].
</Note>

<Note>
  **Isolation:** Verifications can only interact with
  resources inside its [space][1].
</Note>

<Note>
  **Model:** The analysis of the relevant characteristics
  is powered by a specific version of a [verification model][9].
</Note>

<Note>
  **Score:** Confidence in a positive result
  is represented by the [score][5].
</Note>

## Types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Identity" icon="id-card">
    [Identity verifications][6] analyze the similarity between
    the biometric characteristics of an [audio][2] and
    those encapsulated by a [voiceprint][4] to validate that
    they belong to the same [identity][3].
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Authenticity" icon="robot-astromech">
    [Authenticity verifications][7] analyze several characteristics
    related to the authenticity and liveliness of an [audio][2]
    to validate that the voice belongs to a human speaking live.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Message" icon="message-lines">
    [Message verifications][8] analyze the content of an [audio][2]
    to validate that the message found matches the one expected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
